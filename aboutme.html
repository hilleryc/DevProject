About Me

I am a recent graduate from Purdue University in Acoustical Engineering with a minor in Electrical and Computer Engineering. Through school and part-time work I have experience in sound, acoustical, and basic electrical design. Two projects that I worked on in school and work at the Purdue Envision Center initialized my interest in sound synthesis and procedural audio and is something I hope to continue working with.

I completed relevant course work in noise control, theatre sound systems, theatre audio design, advanced C programming, digital system design, signals and systems, and probability as it relates to Electrical Engineering. Throughout this course work in my undergraduate degree I have developed a love of the physics of sound, audio signal flow, sound design, data analysis, and probability.

Prior to attending university, I played percussion since I was about seven years old. I loved practicing and developing skills in snare, marimba, and timpani and being able to hitting different objects or surfaces to make different sounds to create something creative and enjoyable. Combining this with an interest in signal processing and love of math and physics, studying acoustical engineering seemed like a great crossover to find a career path that I would love.
